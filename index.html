University of Cambridge

I am a Lecturer (≈ Assistant Professor) in Natural Language Processing at the University of Edinburgh, where I am part of the Institute for Language, Cognition, and Computation (ILCC), and an Affiliated Lecturer at the University of Cambridge.

I am currently recruiting PhD students and postdocs! For more information, visit the FAQ page.

Previously, I was a visiting postdoctoral scholar at Stanford University and a postdoctoral fellow in computer science at Mila - Quebec AI Institute in Montreal. In 2020, I obtained a PhD in computational linguistics from the University of Cambridge, St John’s College. I interned as AI/ML researcher at Apple in Cupertino.

The main research foci of my lab are:

  • sample efficiency, learning from just a few examples by virtue of inductive biases and knowledge transfer;
  • modular deep learning, designing neural architectures that can disentangle and recombine knowledge through adapters in order to better generalise to new tasks;
  • grounding language learning and use in perception and action;
  • computational typology, using computational tools to model the variation of the world’s languages.

My research earned a Google Research Faculty Award and an ERC Proof of Concept grant on multilingual conversational AI. I received 2 Best Paper Awards at EMNLP 2021 and RepL4NLP 2019. I am a board member of SIGTYP, the ACL special interest group for computational typology, and a scholar of the European Lab for Learning and Intelligent Systems (ELLIS).

Current lab members include:

  • Piotr Nawrot (PhD student), co-supervised with Ivan Titov at the University of Edinburgh.
  • Yifu Qiu (PhD student), co-supervised with Shay Cohen and Anna Korhonen at the University of Edinburgh.
  • Han Zhou (PhD student), co-supervised with Ivan Vulić and Anna Korhonen at the University of Cambridge.
