I will take the position of Lecturer (≈ Assistant Professor) in Natural Language Processing at the University of Edinburgh from August 2022. I am currently recruiting PhD students! If you share similar research interests (take a look at my research statement), consider joining my group. You can apply for a fully funded 4-year PhD with integrated study at the NLP Centre for Doctoral Training or for a fully funded 3-year PhD at the Istitute for Language, Cognition and Computation (ILCC). The deadline is fast approaching.

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow in computer science at Mila - Quebec AI Institute in Montreal and an affiliated researcher at the University of Cambridge. In 2020, I obtained a PhD in computational linguistics from the University of Cambridge, St John’s College. I am also a frequent collaborator of Ryan Cotterell’s lab at ETH Zürich. Previously, I interned as AI/ML researcher at Apple in Cupertino.

My ultimate goal is to reconcile how machines learn and use natural language with the way humans do. This is a source of inspiration to address some of the open challenges in developing technology for hundreds of the world’s languages, which originate from their scarcity of annotated data and their typological variation:

  • sample efficiency, learning from just a few examples by virtue of inductive biases;
  • modularity, disentangling different facets of knowledge and recombining them in original ways in order to better generalise to novel domains;
  • robustness in transferring knowledge between differently distributed domains;
  • grounding language learning and use in perception and action;

My research earned a Google Research Faculty Award and an ERC Proof of Concept grant. I received 2 Best Paper Awards at EMNLP 2021 and RepL4NLP 2019. I had the oppotunity to discuss my research in many invited talks. Finally, I am a board member of SIGTYP, the ACL special interest group for computational typology, and a member of the European Lab for Learning and Intelligent Systems (ELLIS).
